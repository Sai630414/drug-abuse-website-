Beyond Addiction

Comprehensive Case Study on Drug Addiction

Abstract

Drug addiction is an increasing concern across all societal strata, particularly among young people in Bangladesh and beyond. This case study focuses on a 24-year-old male, Delower, who has been addicted to phensidyl for 4.5 years. The addiction, driven by easy access to drugs and peer influence, has affected his physical, mental, and socio-economic well-being. This study highlights the critical need for robust social values, policy reform, and community-driven programs to combat drug abuse effectively.

Introduction

Drug abuse has far-reaching implications on a nation’s socio-economic structure. Bangladesh, due to its location between the "Golden Triangle" (Myanmar, Thailand, Laos) and "Golden Crescent" (Pakistan, Afghanistan, Iran), serves as a critical transit point for drug trafficking. Easy accessibility via land, sea, and air routes exacerbates the problem, leading to an increase in addiction, especially among the youth aged 18–30. Among the most abused substances are cannabis, alcohol, phensidyl, heroin, and sedatives.

History of the Case

Delower, a 24-year-old male, was introduced to drugs by friends during his teenage years. Starting with cannabis, his addiction escalated to phensidyl, a cough syrup containing addictive components like promethazine, codeine, and ephedrine. He consumes phensidyl four times a day, spending approximately Tk. 300–500 daily. To fund his addiction, he resorts to borrowing money or stealing household items.

Physically, Delower appears emaciated, with symptoms of mild anemia and jaundice. Psychologically, he exhibits irritability, slowed speech, and a general disinterest in work or social interactions when deprived of drugs. Despite these struggles, he continues his addictive behavior due to dependency.

Phensidyl and Its Impacts

Composition

Each 5mL of phensidyl contains:

  • Promethazine Hydrochloride: 3.6 mg
  • Codeine: 9.0 mg
  • Ephedrine Hydrochloride: 7.2 mg

Effects and Risks

Phensidyl offers temporary euphoria and relaxation but comes with severe risks such as sedation, hallucinations, gastrointestinal issues, respiratory problems, and cardiovascular complications. Long-term use leads to tolerance, requiring higher doses to achieve the same effect, further endangering health.

Stages of Addiction

Drug addiction evolves in stages:

  1. Habituation: Psychological dependency due to repeated use.
  2. Dependence: Physical reliance characterized by withdrawal symptoms upon cessation.
  3. Tolerance: Increased doses required over time, leading to potential overdose or death.

Addiction is a chronic disorder that impacts the individual’s mental, physical, and social well-being.

Recommendations

To address drug addiction effectively, the following measures are recommended:

  • Policy Reform: Strengthening anti-drug laws and rooting out corruption within law enforcement and narcotics control agencies.
  • Community Engagement: Educational institutions, NGOs, and community leaders should lead awareness and rehabilitation initiatives.
  • Access Control: Restricting over-the-counter sales of addictive substances.
  • Medical Support: Providing treatment facilities focusing on withdrawal management and psychological counseling.
  • Family and Social Values: Promoting a supportive environment to discourage drug use and encourage rehabilitation.

Conclusion

Drug addiction is a pervasive issue with profound personal and societal consequences. Addressing it requires a multi-pronged approach involving legal reform, community engagement, and robust healthcare systems. Through concerted efforts, it is possible to mitigate the impact of addiction, enabling affected individuals like Delower to regain their lives and contribute positively to society.
